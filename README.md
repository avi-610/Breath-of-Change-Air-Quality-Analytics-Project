# Breath of Change: Air Quality Assessment & Community Analytics
Data-driven environmental analytics project assessing air quality awareness, pollution mitigation efforts and performance benchmarking across 45 RWAs and 12 Delhi pollution hotspots.

**Overview**
Government-supported environmental analytics project aligned with air quality awareness and community engagement initiatives under the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change (MoEFCC), Government of India.
The initiative was undertaken to support community-driven air quality improvement through data-driven assessment, citizen engagement, and environmental awareness activities. The project evaluated 45 Resident Welfare Associations (RWAs) across 12 pollution hotspots using survey analytics, field observations, and a weighted scoring framework to benchmark environmental performance and identify improvement opportunities.

**Business Problem**
While city-level air quality data is available, there is limited visibility into community-level pollution sources, awareness and mitigation efforts. This project was designed to assess localized pollution challenges and develop a structured framework for measuring environmental performance across communities.

* Performed data cleaning, transformation, and analysis to ensure accuracy and reliability of findings.
* Generated analytical reports with actionable insights and recommendations to support pollution mitigation initiatives..

**Methodology**
Survey Collection → Data Cleaning → Scoring Framework Development → Performance Benchmarking → Insight Generation

**Key Metrics**
* 12 Pollution Hotspots Assessed
* 45 RWAs Evaluated
* 25,029 Direct Participants Reached
* 120,000+ Digital Outreach

**Key Findings**
* Traffic congestion, road dust and construction activities were major pollution contributors.
* Communities with stronger awareness initiatives achieved higher performance scores.
* Green cover and sustainable mobility practices positively influenced environmental outcomes.
